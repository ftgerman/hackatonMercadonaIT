Tarta de queso, also known as cheesecake, is a popular dessert that originated in Europe. The recipe typically consists of a crust made from graham cracker crumbs or pastry dough, topped with a mixture of cream cheese, sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract, and baked until set.

Here's a basic recipe for Tarta de queso:

Ingredients:
- 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 6 tablespoons (84g) unsalted butter, melted
- 16 ounces (450g) cream cheese, softened
- 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
- 4 large eggs, separated
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:
1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
2. Prepare the crust by mixing the graham cracker crumbs and sugar in a bowl. Stir in the melted butter until well combined.
3. Press the crust mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan.
4. Bake the crust for 10-12 minutes, or until lightly browned.
5. In a large mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth.
6. Add the granulated sugar and beat until combined.
7. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
8. Pour the cheesecake batter into the prepared pan over the crust.
9. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
10. Let the cheesecake cool completely in the pan before refrigerating it for at least 4 hours.

This recipe provides a classic version of Tarta de queso that yields a creamy and delicious dessert. Feel free to experiment with different flavors, such as adding lemon zest or using different types of cheese, to create your own unique variations.
